Ease of use

One of the biggest hurdles of learning computer science, is overcoming undecipherable error messages from the computer. Towards solving this problem, I designed my own minimal programming language called Pipeline, with a primary goal of ease of learning, while still machine-learning capable. You can write software in Pipeline from the comfort of your favorite browser: https://cs-fair.com/shell/

Ask an expert

Even though I designed Pipeline to minimize the opportunity for error messages, they still arise—particularly in the beginning. To handle this inevitability, I engineered a feature into CS-Fair.com that allows students to take snapshots of their programming sessions, and then share their sessions with experts over the web. Experts can quickly identify bugs in student software, and send back guidance.

Viability as an independent business

After trying out CS-Fair.com with a few friends, I learned I had underestimated the work required for an expert to field help requests. After doing some back of the envelope calculations, I determined that to be profitable at scale we would probably have to charge more than schools would be willing to pay for. The work is just to expensive.

But, if we could enable peer-to-peer tutoring, students could mutually benefit, while experts could still chime in when really necessary. This is Blender!
