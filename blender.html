Blender

Real-time remote peer-to-peer tutoring

A story

Imagine you’re using Khan Academy to learn algebra—but you’ve been stuck for the past 10 minutes trying to write the equation for a parabola for a particular problem. You decide it’s time to ask for help, so you click a link at the bottom of the problem that says “request peer help.”

An icon spins indicating that our system has dispatched your help request and is waiting for help to arrive. Seconds later, you hear a voice: “This problem’s pretty tricky. Can you tell me where you’re at with the problem?”

Then you two work together for a few minutes, each using a $60 Wacom Tablet to share notes.

How it works

I've developed a proof-of concept Chrome extension that adds “request peer help” links to Khan Academy web pages. The server receives clicks to those links, and dispatches those clicks to other students who have indicated they are available for tutoring at the moment.

Since all of our participating students have installed the Chrome extension for Khan Academy, we can also automatically keep track of the progress of each student. When we receive a request for help, we might choose to pair the requester with a tutor who has recently just demonstrated mastery of the material.
