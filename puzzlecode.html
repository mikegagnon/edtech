High-density cyber bombs

While hacking for GMU, MIT, Harvard, and DARPA, I cultivated an expertise in high-density cyber bombs. Basically, the way it works is you trick your victim’s server into performing very slow computations, which clog the server’s CPU, which prevents other users from receiving responses from the server.

Middle-school hackers

Several years ago, I presented a 20-minute lesson that introduced high-density cyber bombs to several groups of middle-school students who had never programmed before. Then, I invited them to try to crash my server, by tricking it into performing heavyweight computations. They happily succeeded.
