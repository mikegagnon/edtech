The Magic Textbook

Schools want to throw textbooks out the window, because they’re terrible. But, what if they were magical?

What if your textbook was thoroughly and seamlessly integrated with interactive content, videos, animations, discussion forums, peer tutoring, expert tutoring, quizzes, tests, self assessments, and data analytics to automatically assess proficiency, competency, and mastery? What if your textbook provided you the opportunity to optionally deep dive into areas of special interest? What if you could contribute to your textbook?

Such a textbook would deserve to serve as the backbone of instruction and the workhorse behind learning. What if we could delight students, teachers, and parents, alike?

Proposal

Let’s compete toe to toe with Khan Academy. Whereas Khan Academy has been focusing on quantity over quality, we can out compete them on quality from the start, and then expand out our curriculum once we have mastered the art of magical learning.

To be more concrete, I propose we begin by rapidly developing and launching a magical textbook on a subject that is high in demand, low in supply, and an area of my personal expertise: computer science.

To be even more concrete, I propose we offer our first textbook to high school students who have never programmed before, who lack contact with in-person expertise, and we mediate learning JavaScript—the lingua franca of the Web. By the conclusion of the book we will have programmed a chess AI together.

This will be a challenge. It’s has always been a challenge to teach computer science over the web. But if we can pull this off first, we will have paved the way for other subjects.
